About Me

AI researcher focused on ensemble LLMs and unstructured text categorization.

Background

I hold a Master’s degree in Computer Science from UC Davis, where my work focused on ensemble methods for large language models (LLMs) and how they can be used to interpret and classify unstructured text more effectively. My research intersects natural language processing, applied AI, and system design for collaborative inference.

Before and during my academic journey, I’ve built tools and experiments that explore how multiple language models can be coordinated for better reasoning, accuracy, and adaptability across noisy, real-world data.

Skills & Interests

  • Ensemble LLM Architecture and Evaluation
  • Unstructured Text Classification
  • Large-Scale Prompting and Multi-Agent AI
  • Python, PyTorch, Transformers, Scikit-learn
  • OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta LLM APIs
  • Research Prototyping and Paper Writing

Personal Note

While most of my work is technical, I care deeply about clarity, transparency, and making powerful tools accessible to others. Whether it’s through research, writing, or the occasional side project, I’m always looking for ways to push the boundaries of what’s possible with LLM collaboration—while keeping the focus on real-world results and thoughtful design.
