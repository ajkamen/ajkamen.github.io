About Me

AI researcher specializing in LLM evaluation, ensemble reasoning, and unstructured text categorization.

Background

I hold a Master’s degree in Computer Science from UC Davis, where my research focused on evaluating large language models, developing ensemble reasoning methods, and improving cross-model agreement for unstructured text categorization. My work spans natural language processing, multi-model collaboration, and system design for scalable AI inference.

I developed a mathematical framework and evaluation methodology for cross-model categorization, as well as an ensemble-LLM architecture that achieved more than 65% improvement over single-model outputs. This research led to two publications and a pending U.S. patent on multi-model LLM categorization mechanisms.

Research

My research centers on three areas:

  • LLM evaluation, error analysis, and comparative benchmarking
  • Ensemble reasoning and collaborative multi-model inference
  • Applied NLP for large-scale categorization and analytics

I combine experimental research with engineering-focused system design, exploring how multiple models can work together through structured decision-making to improve accuracy, robustness, and interpretability on real-world data.
